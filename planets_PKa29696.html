Mercury

  • It is the closest planet to the sun.
  • It is named after the Roman messenger of gods because of its fast movements around the Sun.
  • It is the smallest and innermost planet in the solar system.
  • Its mean diameter is 4,880 km.
  • It may appear as a bright star.
  • It was called as a morning star and an evening star because of its apparitions.
  • Its appearance is very similar with Earth's moon.
  • It has just 38% the gravity of Earth.
  • It is covered with holes.
  • Its surface was shaped by impact cratering, which is when objects (like comets or meteoroids) from space hits its surface.
  • Although it is the closest planet to the sun, it is not the hottest planet of solar system. Its day temperature reaches 427°C and night temperature reaches -183°C.
  • It can be seen with naked eye.
  • It does not have any moons and rings.
  • Two spacecrafts have visited Mercury:
    Mariner10

    Mariner 10 was an American robotic space probe launched by NASA on 3 November 1973 to fly on Mercury and Venus.

    and
    MESSENGER

    MESSENGER was a NASA robotic space probe launched on 3 August 2004, that orbited the planet Mercury between 2011 and 2015.

  • As there is no atmosphere and water, no life is possible on this planet.

Venus

  • It is the second planet of the solar system which is next to mercury.
  • It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.
  • It is the brightest planet of the solar system.
  • Its mean diameter is 12,100 km.
  • It can be seen with naked eye.
  • Atmosphere on Venus contains 95% of carbon-dioxide.
  • This planet is also called earth's sister because it is similar to Earth in size and mass.
  • There is no possiblity of life on this planet.
  • It takes longer to rotate about its axis than any other planet in the solar system with the revolution period of 243 Earth days. In other words, a day on Venus is longer than a year.
  • Unlike the other planets, Venus spins clockwise on its axis.
  • It does not have any moons or satellites.
  • It has been made sacred to gods of many cultures, and has been a prime inspiration for writers and poets as the "morning star" and the "evening star".
  • Much of the Venusian surface appears to have been shaped by volcanic activity.
  • Despite Venus is not closest to the sun, it has the highest temperature reaches 471°Celsius.
  • It reflects 70% of all the sunlight it receives. Therefore, it shines so brightly.

Earth

  • Next to Venus is the third planet, Earth.
  • It is the fifth largest planet of the solar system.
  • Its mean diameter is 12,756 km.
  • It is surrounded by gaseous envelope called atmosphere.
  • At a height of 30 to 50 km from the surface of earth, a layer called ozone layers is present. It is also called ozone blanket.
  • The outermost layer of earth is called crust.
  • Life is possible on earth because its atmosphere contains water and oxygen, and suitable temperature exists on it.
  • It consists of oceans, rivers, and soil.
  • Its soil is responsible for the growth of vegetations and habitat for land creatures.
  • Its atmosphere consists mostly of nitrogen and oxygen.
  • It has a powerful magnetic field.
  • Its natural satellite is Moon.
  • Its shape is nearly spherical.
  • Its polar regions are covered with ice.
  • Its rotation is gradually slowing at approximately 17 milliseconds/100 years.

Mars

  • Next to Earth, Mars is the fourth planet.
  • It is named after the Roman god of war.
  • It is the planet which is nearest to the earth.
  • Its mean diameter is 6,878 km.
  • It can be seen with the naked eye.
  • It is reddish planet of solar system, because it is covered with red dust causing the atmosphere and soil to look red.
  • Atmosphere on mars contains mainly carbon-dioxide
  • No life has been detected so far on this planet, but many missions are determining Mars' past and future potential for life.
  • There are two theories about its creation.
    1. The first theory is core accretion.
    2. The second is the disk instability model.
  • It has the largest dust storms in the solar system.
  • The Sun appears about half the size on Mars.
  • The sunsets on Mars are blue.
  • It has two moons named Phobos and Deimos.
  • It has huge amount of iron-rich minerals which give Mars the bright rust color.

Jupiter

  • Next to Mars, Jupiter is the fifth planet.
  • It is named after the Roman god of sky and thunder.
  • It is the heaviest and the biggest planet of solar system.
  • Its mean diameter is 1,42,984 km. It has a thin ring system.
  • It can be seen with naked eye.
  • Atmosphere of Jupiter contains poisonous gases, so life is impossible on it.
  • It has a rocky core of heavier elements, but like the other giant planets, Jupiter lacks a well-defined solid surface.
  • Its upper atmosphere is about 88-92% hydrogen and 8-12% helium by percent volume of gas molecules.
  • Its mass is 2.5 times that of all the other planets in the solar system.
  • The temperature and pressure inside Jupiter increase steadily inward, due to the Kelvin-Helmholtz mechanism.
  • It has the largest planetary atmosphere in the Solar System (5,000 km in altitude).
  • Its magnetic field is fourteen times as strong as that of Earth.
  • It has 79 natural moons. Jupiter's moon Ganymede is the largest moon in the solar system.
  • Nine spacecrafts have visited Jupiter.

Saturn

  • Next to Jupiter, Saturn is the sixth planet.
  • It is named after the Roman god of agriculture and harvest.
  • It is the second biggest and heaviest planet of solar system.
  • Its mean diameter is 1,20,536 kms.
  • It can be seen with naked eye.
  • Life is not possible on this planet.
  • It has beautiful rings which are made up of ice, rocks and dust. They can be seen even with an inexpensive telescope.
  • It is named after the Roman god of wealth and agriculture.
  • It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine times that of Earth.
  • It is the only planet of the Solar System that is less dense than water - about 30% less.
  • It could float in water because it is mostly made of gas.
  • The outer atmosphere of Saturn contains 96.3% molecular hydrogen and 3.25% helium by volume.
  • It has an intrinsic magnetic field that has a simple, symmetric shape - a magnetic dipole.
  • It has 82 moons. Its Titan moon has a significant atmosphere (that is denser than any of the terrestrial planets except Venus) as compared to other moons in the solar system.
  • Four spacecrafts have visited Saturn.

Uranus

  • This seventh planet is next to Saturn.
  • It is named after the Greek god of the sky.
  • Its mean diameter is 51,000 km.
  • It cannot be seen with naked eye.
  • This planet rotates on its axis from east to west.
  • It is covered with thick layer of beautiful blue and green gases as a result of the methane in its mostly hydrogen-helium atmosphere.
  • It is composed of rocks and various ices.
  • It has bands of clouds that blow around it rapidly. But they are extremely faint, visible only with radical image enhancement.
  • It has a ring system, a magnetosphere, and numerous moons.
  • Its structure consists of three layers: a rocky core in the centre, an icy mantle in the middle and an outer gaseous hydrogen/helium envelope.
  • It hits the coldest temperatures of any planet (about -224°Celsius).
  • It has 27 natural satellites.
  • One rotation on Uranus takes about 17 hours.
  • The summer season on Uranus is two decades of non-stop sunlight, and the winter season has also the same time interval in total darkness.

Neptune

  • It is eighth planet of our solar system.
  • It is named after the Roman god of sea.
  • Its diameter is 49,528 km.
  • It can not be seen with naked eye; however, it can be seen with binoculars (but a large telescope is needed to see anything other than a tiny disk).
  • It spins on its axis very rapidly.
  • It is the third most massive planet and the densest giant planet.
  • The core of Neptune is likely composed of iron, nickel and silicates.
  • Its atmosphere is divided into two main regions: the lower troposphere, where temperature decreases with altitude, and the stratosphere, where temperature increases with altitude.
  • It has 14 moons.Triton is the largest Neptunian moon.
  • It has planetary ring system.
  • It is the smallest gas and ice giants.
  • It has 6 faint rings.
  • The atmosphere of Neptune is made of hydrogen and helium, with some methane. These components give Neptune blue color.
  • Its weather is characterised by extremely dynamic storm systems.
