ANSWERS:

  1. NASA estimated that there are at least 100 billion planets in our Milky Way alone. Others estimated that the Milky Way galaxy might have anywhere between 100 to 200 billion planets.
  2. Mercury.
  3. The inner four rocky planets at the center of the solar system are:
    • Mercury
    • Venus
    • Earth
    • Mars
  4. Terrestrial planets are made of silicate rocks or metals. These are also known as telluric planets or rocky planets. Their structure is similar to that of the earth.
  5. Terrestrial Planets are:
    • Mercury
    • Venus
    • Earth
    • Mars
  6. A planet is known as inferior or interior planet if its orbit lies inside the other planet's orbit around the sun. In this situation, the latter planet is said to be superior to the former.
  7. In the reference frame of Earth, the inferior planets are Mercury and Venus, while the superior planets are Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.
  8. Dwarf planet is a planetry-mass object that does not dominate its region of space and is not a satellite. That is, it is in direct orbit of the Sun and is massive enough to be plastic - for its gravity to maintain it in a hydrostatically equilibrious shape (usually a spheroid).
  9. Jovian planets are primilarly made of hydrogen and helium. These planets have large number of moons and have ring system around them. Their structure is similar to Jupiter. Jovian planets are:
    • Jupiter
    • Saturn
    • Uranus
    • Neptune
  10. The surface conditions, like temperature and gravity, of Jovian planets are such that life on these planets can never be possible.
  11. Moon.
  12. Jupiter.
  13. Jupiter. It has 82 moons in number.
  14. Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and Earth is much smaller than Jupiter. It is estimated that 1,300 Earths could fit inside of Jupiter.
  15. Neptune is the coldest planet because it is the eighth planet in our solar system and therefore the furthest away from the Sun. The Sun is the primary heat source so it would make sense that the planet with the greatest distance from it would be the coldest.
